César Costa Songs and Albums

Costa started his singing career at the age of 17 as César Roel singing and playing guitar with the rock-and-roll band Los Black Jeans, singing Spanish versions of American hit singles as well as original songs like La batalla de Jericó.

They recorded their first album in 1959 with Peerless Records. His backup singer was world-renowned tenor Plácido Domingo. When the band signed with Musart Records the band changed its name to Los Camisas Negras and César Roel to Cesar Costa.

Soon after, the band disintegrated and César started a successful solo career, recording three albums with Orfeón Records. Then with RCA Mexicana, he recorded romantic songs. In 1983 he participated in the OTI Festival and obtained second place with a song by Sergio Andrade titled Tierno.

César Costa Unicef

César Costa was the first Mexican to receive the title of UNICEF Ambassador. Since his appointment in 2004, he has demonstrated an unrivaled commitment to children’s rights. In addition, he has been a member of the Advisory Council of UNICEF Mexico since its foundation in 1995 and his help has been essential in establishing new alliances in the country.

César Costa Movies and TV Shows

Supported by his success in the music industry, he joined other singers in films of the Cinema of Mexico such as Angélica María, Enrique Guzmán, and Alberto Vázquez. In 1961 he made his first film Juventud Rebelde.

In 1986 he starred in the sitcom Papa soltero, a show that became a success in Latin America and then was adapted to the screen as Me tengo que casar. In the 1980s he hosted a comedy show “La Carabina De Ambrosio” and in the 1990s a talk show with model Rebecca de Alba called Un Nuevo Día and next year Al Fin de Semana.

In 1993 he presented a show together with Angélica María, Enrique Guzmán, Manolo Muñoz and Alberto Vásquez at the Auditorio Nacional with great success. He also had a radio show called De Costa a Costa broadcast on Cadena ACIR. As of 2005 he has a TV show called Ensalada César on Canal Once of the Instituto Politécnico Nacional.
