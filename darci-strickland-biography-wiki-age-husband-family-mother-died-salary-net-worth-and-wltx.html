Who is Darci Strickland?, Darci Strickland Biography and Wiki

Darci Strickland is an American Anchor and content manager at WLTX-TV in Columbia, SC, where she appears on the station’s daily evening newscasts. There was never any doubt as to what kind of career Darci would have: it had to be communication. Darci’s love of people, infectious smile, and commitment to the community have made her one of the most popular journalists in the Midlands.

A native of Ladson, South Carolina, she joined WLTX-TV in 1997 as a General Assignment Reporter and Weekend Weather Anchor. In the year 1998, she moved to the mornings as the Meteorologist and teamed up with J.R. Berry on News 19’s Morning as well as Noon News programs.

In early the year, 2002 Darci joined J.R. Berry as co-anchor for the 6 as well as 7 PM Newscast. In August of 2007, Darci along with Andrea Mock changed the face of local TV news by starting Friends at Five. Currently, Darci anchors the Noon broadcast, co-anchors Friends at Five with Andrea Mock, as well as co-anchors the 6 pm broadcast with J.R. Berry. In addition to her role as anchor, Darci frequently works on special assignments. She has a degree in Journalism from the University of South Carolina and a certificate in Broadcast Meteorology from Mississippi State University.

Darci’s infectious and positive speaking style makes her WLTX’s most requested speaker, leading to many requests from schools, charitable organizations, churches, as well as other associations statewide. She is the main spokesperson for Buddy Call 19, a station project that encourages women to perform self-breast examinations. In addition to Buddy Call 19, she spearheads the recognition of great educators, through our Teacher of the Week awards.

She has earned Emmys and an Alfred I. DuPont Award as well as Murrow for her work as a journalist. Darci a 2019 University of South Carolina Media Law School Fellow. She is a volunteer Guardian ad Litem with Richland County CASA as well as a Board Member for Workshop Theater.

She is the 2019 South Carolina Broadcaster’s Association Anchor of the Year, 2009 South Carolina Broadcaster’s Association TV Personality of the Year and in the year 2005 University of South Carolina Young Alumni of the Year. She is a member of the National Association of Black Journalists. Darci attends Journey United Methodist Church in Columbia and is a member of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority.

In her spare time, she enjoys reading, traveling, and spending time with her husband Torrey and their daughters Logan, Layla, and Lauren. Her passion for community service is outweighed only by her passion for serving the people of the Midlands through her work at News 19.
